Protecting your pets from worsening air quality

Air quality is affected by vehicular emissions, industrial pollution, deforestation, burning fossil fuels, and improper waste disposal. These pollutants don't just affect humans; they significantly impact our pets.

Why Does It Happen?

Human activities such as excessive use of vehicles and industries release harmful pollutants into the air. Natural factors like dust storms also play a role. Pets, especially dogs and cats, are often closer to the ground, inhaling higher concentrations of these pollutants. Additionally, their respiratory systems can be more sensitive than ours.

How Does It Affect our pets?

Poor air quality can cause respiratory problems like coughing, wheezing, and difficulty breathing in pets. It can also lead to eye and skin irritation, and exacerbate existing heart conditions. Long-term exposure can contribute to chronic illnesses, and can be especially dangerous for senior pets or those with pre-existing conditions.

Preventions

Limit Outdoor Exposure: On days with poor air quality alerts, reduce the time your pets spend outdoors. Schedule walks for early mornings or late evenings when pollution levels are typically lower.

Monitor Air Quality: Stay informed about local air quality indexes. Use apps or websites that provide real-time data.

Provide a Clean Indoor Environment: Use air purifiers with HEPA filters to remove pollutants from indoor air. Regularly vacuum and dust your home.

Avoid Strenuous Activity: Limit intense exercise during periods of poor air quality.

Keep Pets Hydrated: Ensure your pets have access to fresh, clean water. Hydration helps support their respiratory system.

Be Aware of Symptoms: Watch for signs of respiratory distress, such as coughing, wheezing, rapid breathing, or lethargy. If you notice any of these symptoms, consult your veterinarian immediately.

Avoid Smoke and Irritants: Refrain from smoking indoors or near your pets. Avoid using harsh chemicals or cleaning products that emit strong fumes.

Provide Shelter: If your pet spends time outdoors, ensure they have access to a sheltered area where they can escape from dust and pollutants.
