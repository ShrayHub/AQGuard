Precautions for Children and Elderly

Children and the elderly are particularly vulnerable to the harmful effects of poor air quality. Taking necessary precautions can help protect their health and well-being.


Limit Outdoor Activities:
- Encourage children and elderly individuals to stay indoors during high pollution days, especially during peak hours.

Use Air Purifiers:
- Install air purifiers at home to reduce indoor pollution levels.

Stay Hydrated:
- Drinking plenty of water helps the body flush out toxins and supports respiratory health.

Wear Protective Masks:
- Use protective masks, especially when going outside on high pollution days.

Healthy Diet:
- Include antioxidant-rich foods in the diet to strengthen the immune system.

Regular Medical Check-ups:
- Regular health check-ups can help detect and manage pollution-related health issues at an early stage.

Ventilate Homes:
- Ensure proper ventilation at home without exposing the interiors to outside pollution.
